Biography

I am a 20 year old Albanian adult boy who moved to the United States three years ago. Originally I was born in Kosovo, so my home language is Albanian. Now, I am a student at Raritan Valley Community College, and majoring in Information Technology. I've been interested in technology and enjoy learning about new advancements in the field. My passion though, continue to be sports, especially soccer, which has been a significant part of my whole life. I've played for a team back in Kosovo, and I continued to play alone when I moved to the United States. The love for the sport has motivated me to stay active, work hard, and maintain a healthy lifestyle. When I first moved to the United States, I couldn't speak a single word, and still today I feel not adapted to this new environment. Now I've learned English well, and I can speak Albanian fluently. The people here made me not enjoy the state at the most, but I still continue to learn about different cultures. So, I am a very motivated person and passionate about sports and technology. I am not the best for technology, but I try my best to make my family happy since I am the first person in the family who has gone to college. I am committed to my studies and I hope to pursue a career in the IT field, for a better future for myself and make my parents as proud as I can. Being far away from my home country, suffering adapting in a new place, motivated me to continue and try any opportunities the United States can offer me. I am not going to be proud of myself until I can keep a constitency of happiness in my family. So, this is me, Lirik Salihu!

Holmes nodded his head. "The name is familiar to me," said he.

"The family was at one time among the richest in England, and the estates extended over the borders into Berkshire in the north, and Hampshire in the west. In the last century, however, four successive heirs were of a dissolute and wasteful disposition, and the family ruin was eventually completed by a gambler in the days of the Regency. Nothing was left save a few acres of ground, and the two-hundred-year-old house, which is itself crushed under a heavy mortgage. The last squire dragged out his existence there, living the horrible life of an aristocratic pauper; but his only son, my stepfather, seeing that he must adapt himself to the new conditions, obtained an advance from a relative, which enabled him to take a medical degree and went out to Calcutta, where, by his professional skill and his force of character, he established a large practice. In a fit of anger, however, caused by some robberies which had been perpetrated in the house, he beat his native butler to death and narrowly escaped a capital sentence.
